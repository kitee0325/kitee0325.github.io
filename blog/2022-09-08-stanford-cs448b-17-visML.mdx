### Some Guidelines for ML Visualizations

1. Visualizations should align with user expertise
   - Model Developers and Builders
   - Model Users
   - Domain Experts
   - Non-Experts
   - Learners/Students
     ![](https://kitee-1301346990.cos.ap-nanjing.myqcloud.com/Obsidian/202410141602710.png?imageSlim)
2. Provide effective data representations for the task
   - Debugging and Improving Models
   - Comparing and Selecting Models
   - Interpretability and Explainability
   - Teaching ML Concepts
     ![](https://kitee-1301346990.cos.ap-nanjing.myqcloud.com/Obsidian/202410141602972.png?imageSlim)
3. Support understanding of model uncertainty  
   Uncertainty is an inevitable feature of data-driven models in most real-world applications.
   ![](https://kitee-1301346990.cos.ap-nanjing.myqcloud.com/Obsidian/202410141603398.png?imageSlim)
4. Exploit interactivity and promote rich interactions
   - Editing data points
   - Evaluating Hypotheses
   - Constructing Explanations
     ![](https://kitee-1301346990.cos.ap-nanjing.myqcloud.com/Obsidian/202410141603440.png?imageSlim)
5. Support expressive inputs
   - Direct Manipulation
   - Query-by-demonstration
     ![](https://kitee-1301346990.cos.ap-nanjing.myqcloud.com/Obsidian/202410141604339.png?imageSlim)
